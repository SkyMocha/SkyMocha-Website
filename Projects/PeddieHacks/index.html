Peddie Hacks 2020 & 2021

A school-hosted Hackathon

Overview

PeddieHacks is a yearly hackathon hosted by The Peddie School. The Hackathon started shortly before COVID, so for its first two years it has been online.

PeddieHacks 2020

During the 2019-2020 school year, my schools Computer Science club began planning an in-person Hackathon; held in our schools science center, we would invite all participants from the surrounding schools to gether on these two floors to create. However, amidst a global pandemic, we decided to make it an online Hackathon from August 22nd to August 23rd, 2020. The Hackathon was primarily hosted over Discord, where I was a communicator, as well as administrator and organizer.

Day one was primarily the opening, and things went smoothly as it was primarily the opening ceremony and brainstorming for teams; however, as the end approached on day two things got hectic very quickly leading to a 15 hour work day. During the second day I was tasked at the start (9 AM) to lead a game session, and later throughout the day was tasked on things such as checking in with teams, creating a judging schedule, making announcements, and more. The greatest challenge was organizing the judging; as judging was supposed to be approximately 3 to 4 hours, but ended up lasting almost 7 full hours. I was for the majority of those 7 hours constantly adjusting the schedule to accomodate teams and help judges.

In the end I only survived thanks to lots of caffeine.

Peddie Hacks 2021

For 2021 Peddie Hacks, I took a more backseat approach to the organization. After the hell that was 2020, I wanted a more relaxing experience. However, I did serve during the orginazation process by making suggestions by attending the organization zoom meetings. Most of my suggestions served to help create the judging criteria.

During the actual competition, my biggest roles were once again being the MC and organizing the Discord server. I updated the 2020 PeddieHacks Discord bot, documented it, and put it on GitHub for future students to use. This was vital in organizing teams, giving them rooms to work, and checking in with them.

When it came to being master of ceremonies, I ran the latter half of the opening cermonies, and the entire closing cermonies. I definitiely did not do my best during the closing ceremonies. I was given the slides three minutes before go time, so I made a lot of mistakes, but cold reading is my specialty.
