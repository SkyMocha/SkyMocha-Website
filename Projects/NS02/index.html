A Music Visualizer and Remote Control for Speakers

My first term of Sophomore Year I was tasked with a open-ended project using a Raspberry Pi. The final result is a remote for a speaker. The program runs a Python (Flask) backend, connected to a web-page remote, containing a music visualizer, as well as a way to choose the currently playing song.

The remote is paired to a Raspberry Pi connected to a custom-built speaker. The speaker does not light up as intended, yet produces sound. It is made of 2 sheets of Balsa wood, with Dayton Audio exciters connected in parallel.

At the end of the term, I was asked to give a presentation on the project, which can be seen on the right.
